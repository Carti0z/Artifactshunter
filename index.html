    About Us

    ArtifactHunter has been providing quality heritage management services since 1992. Our firm is committed to collaborating with local First Nation Organizations, municipalities, and the Provincial Archaeology Branch to offer you accountable and effective management strategies.

    We primarily work with municipalities, developers, private land-owners and First Nation Groups on a variety of projects involving archaeological assessments.

    MiSSION

    Foster collaboration and engage with indigenous and local communities, respecting their perspectives, knowledge, and connections to archaeological sites.

    VISION

    Uncover and interpret the material remains of past human societies to reconstruct and understand the breadth and depth of human history.

    Stratigraphic Notation

    02

    Typological Symbols

    Depending on the artifact type, specific symbols or abbreviations may be used. For example, a shorthand for pottery styles, lithic types, or architectural features.

    Numbers or alphanumeric codes are assigned to catalog artifacts, providing a unique identifier for each item.

    Various symbols are used to describe and categorize different motifs and elements.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    What is archaeology?

    Archaeology is the study of human history and prehistory through the excavation and analysis of artifacts, structures, and other physical remains. It aims to reconstruct and understand past human societies.

    How do archaeologists know where to dig?

    Archaeologists use various methods, including surveys, aerial photography, and geophysical techniques, to identify potential archaeological sites. Historical documents, local knowledge, and previous research also guide excavation decisions.

    What is the difference between anthropology and archaeology?

    Anthropology is the broader study of humans, encompassing cultural anthropology, physical anthropology, linguistic anthropology, and archaeology. Archaeology specifically focuses on studying past human cultures through material remains.

    How are artifacts dated?

    Artifacts are dated using various methods, including radiocarbon dating, dendrochronology (tree-ring dating), stratigraphy (examining layers of soil), and thermoluminescence (measuring trapped electrons in ceramics).

    Can anyone become an archaeologist?

    Yes, anyone with a passion for archaeology can pursue a career in the field. It often involves obtaining a relevant education, such as a degree in archaeology or anthropology, and gaining practical experience through fieldwork.

    What challenges do archaeologists face during excavations?

    Challenges include funding constraints, logistical issues, ethical considerations, and the need to balance excavation goals with site preservation. Weather conditions and unexpected discoveries can also pose challenges.
